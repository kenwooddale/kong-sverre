Ancestry                                          



Both my maternal and paternal sides of the family immigrated to Kendall County, Illinois near Chicago from Norway in the mid 1800s, some by way of Minnesota.  They were farmers. Probably, like most immigrates, started with very little. Records indicate many were prosperous farmers and leaders in the community.  I was told my parents had considered the name Kendall for me.



One nice June weekend in 1990 Terri and I decided to drive to northern Illinois and see if we could find information about Norwegian immigrants.

Members of both sides of my family have done extensive genealogy research – convenient for me.


The Farm

I was born and raised on an Iowa farm, until attending a technical school in Chicago after high school. I liked the farm life. My Dad had a small to average sized farm. I was the oldest of three boys, and helped with a lot of farm work. Probably drove a tractor before age six. Farm work was hard, but we always seemed to have nice clothes and cars.


Chicago

Chicago was a real experience, and a great change from the farm and small town. I had a room with two other guys in the house of a real nice Irish family. I went to school and worked part time. Everybody worked. The school had a housing and employment office. If you didn’t like your part time job, you quit and got another.


Chicago was an electronics center. Many of us students worked part time at electronics companies. One of the nation’s largest electronics retail stores was Allied Radio in Chicago. I enjoyed spending a couple hours there on Saturday afternoons. You could get almost anywhere in Chicago on public transportation. There were several restaurants within easy walking distance from the house.


Some weekends my roommate and I rode the bus and “L” (elevated rail & subway) to downtown Chicago. I just loved downtown Chicago. Ron and I might stop at a $1.99 stake house, and then a movie in one of the majestic old downtown theaters.


In those days Chicago was known as “The city that works” – clean and little crime. Mayor Richard J Daily ruled with an iron hand.


KCRG

After completing a communications course at DeVry, I worked at a TV station in Cedar Rapids. That was fun. It was all weekend and evening work. I was a cameraman and weekend studio supervisor. Fun being around the talent during working and after hours. After two years, I wanted more of a challenge.
 
Return to School

I returned to DeVry for a degree program. I had my car there, and shared an apartment with three other guys. I had money saved up, so didn’t need to work part time. Didn’t use the car much - maybe to the beach in the summertime.


McDonnell Aircraft

McDonnell Aircraft was hiring lots of people in the early sixties. I worked in the same general building complex for 35 years under different company names. I liked the work, and for the most part, the people. Both Boeing and ISSC (IBM) offered an early retirement. I needed something to do in retirement.


Volunteering

One of my longtime coworkers at Boeing (bless his heart) introduced me to Wings of Hope. I learned to fly at Lambert Field in the early seventies, and always had an interest in aviation. So, volunteering at an aviation charity was a good fit. I volunteered for over twenty years at Wings, and really enjoyed the work and the people.


Flying

Terri and I bought an airplane in July 1999. We had it about eighteen years, and took it (VFR) almost to the East coast and almost to the West coast.  I went into Albuquerque International, Nashville International, and Chicago Midway among others. We made over thirty trips to Iowa.


I liked the one and two overnight trips we took in the Cherokee. I hardly ever made hotel/motel reservations – tried to avoid get-there-itis. Travel in a GA aircraft was much more pleasant than flying commercial. FBOs were friendly and usually helped with overnight accommodations. They sometimes made reservations and arranged transportation – maybe a van or crew car (free). There was usually a pilot’s lounge with vending machines, etc. Of course, we could only travel during nice weather. I waited about six weeks before we launched on our first of three trips to Durango, Colorado to visit relatives. We made over thirty trips to Iowa to visit my parents.


Radio

I love radio. I built a crystal radio with a cat's whisker detector and a 150’ antenna on the farm. I think I could receive two or three stations. My brother and I built a Knight-kit Wireless Broadcaster from Allied Radio. We had 2 turntables, and could broadcast about a mile. I also built one of the first Citizen’s Band Radios (17W7183) in the area. Years ago, when traveling on the road, I would much prefer to listen to a local AM radio station.  AM radio has sure gone down hill today.
